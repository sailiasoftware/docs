A **hire** is the right session type for renting equipment or facilities by the hour, half-day, or day. This guide walks through the hire setup wizard end to end.

If you're not sure whether you need a course or a hire, see [courses vs hire](/activities/courses-vs-hire) first.

## Step 1: Choose session type

Start a new session and select **Hire**. This tells Sailia the booking is for a time-based rental rather than a taught course.

## Step 2: Basic hire information

| Field | What to set |
| --- | --- |
| **Item name** | A clear, descriptive name (e.g. *Single kayak*, *Bike hire*) |
| **Hire increment** | The smallest bookable time unit, in minutes |
| **Price per increment** | The cost for one increment — total price scales with the number of increments booked |
| **Colour** | Used on the calendar to distinguish hire types |
| **Participants per ticket** | How many people each ticket covers (e.g. 2 for a double kayak) |

### Choosing the right hire increment

The hire increment defines what durations customers can book. Choose the increment that matches how customers actually use your equipment.

| Increment | Bookings allowed | Good for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **30 minutes** | 30 min, 1 hr, 1.5 hr, ... | Short rentals with flexible durations |
| **60 minutes** | 1 hr, 2 hr, 3 hr, ... | Standard equipment rentals |
| **90 minutes** | 1.5 hr, 3 hr, 4.5 hr, ... | Activities that naturally take 90 minutes |

A 60-minute increment priced at £50 means: 2 hours = £100, 3 hours = £150, and so on.

## Step 3: Select activities

Tag the hire with every relevant activity so customers find it when browsing by activity type. Use **Create activity** if a tag doesn't exist.

## Step 4: Booking settings

| Setting | What it controls |
| --- | --- |
| **Booking method** | Online and phone, or phone-only |
| **Minimum notice period** | How far in advance the booking must be made |
| **Minimum participants** | Smallest party size on one booking |
| **Maximum participants** | Largest party size on one booking |
| **Ask for date of birth** | Whether to collect a DOB during booking |

## Step 5: Resources required

### Qualifications (staff)

Most self-service hires don't need staff qualifications. For supervised or instructed hires, add the qualification and ratio — for example, *1 safety supervisor per 5 participants*.

### Kit (equipment)

List the equipment included with the hire (kayak, paddle, life jacket) and the [kit ratio](/activities/activities#kit-and-resources). For hire items, the default is **not shared between bookings** — each booking gets its own equipment.

### Add-ons (optional extras)

Link any [add-ons](/activities/addon-setup) customers can buy alongside — extended time, additional equipment, insurance, delivery.

## Step 6: Connections

| Connection | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **Waivers** | Liability documents required before the hire. Most hires need at least one. See [waivers](/customers/waivers). |
| **Communication groups** | Controls which [automated emails](/customers/notifications) fire for this hire. |

## Step 7: Hire images

Upload photos showing the equipment, key features, and what the customer can expect. The first image is the main one shown in listings. See [uploading images](/getting-started/uploading-images) for formats and dimensions.

## Step 8: Hire description

Cover:

- What the item is and its specifications
- What's included with the hire
- Restrictions (age limits, experience needed)
- Safety information
- Pickup and return procedure

## Step 9: Review and save

The wizard shows a summary before save. Pay particular attention to the hire increment and price — those are the easiest things to get wrong and are the hardest to change retrospectively. Click **Save** when you're happy.

## What to do next

1. Open **What's On** and add the availability windows when the item can be hired.
2. Add additional ticket types (half day, full day, member price) if needed.
3. Make a test booking to verify pricing and time slots look right.
4. Share your booking page with customers.

## Tips

- Set an increment that matches typical bookings. Too short (15 min) confuses customers; too long (4 hr) limits flexibility.
- Pair a short increment with a [tiered discount](/pricing/discount-codes) to make longer rentals cheaper per hour.
- Add ticket types for common durations (*Half day - 4 hours*, *Full day - 8 hours*) to nudge customers towards better-value bookings.

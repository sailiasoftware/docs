Activities are the core bookable items in Sailia. You create activities to represent anything customers can reserve — from surf lessons and kayak hires to multi-day events. Each activity has its own schedule, pricing, capacity, and staffing configuration.

## Activity types

Sailia supports three types of activities:

| Type | Use case |
| --- | --- |
| **Courses** | Scheduled sessions with a fixed start and end time, such as a beginner surf lesson or a yoga class |
| **Hires** | Equipment or resource rentals for a defined duration, such as kayak hire or wetsuit rental |
| **Events** | One-off or recurring events with attendee management, such as a beach clean-up or open day |

All three types share the same booking and payment flow. The main differences are in how scheduling and capacity work.

## Create an activity

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the activities section">
    Navigate to your activities dashboard and select **Create Activity**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the activity type">
    Select whether this is a course, hire, or event.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the basics">
    Enter the activity name, description, and any images. This information appears on your booking page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure scheduling">
    Set when the activity runs — start times, duration, and recurrence. For hires, define the available time slots.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set pricing">
    Define the base price per person or per booking. You can add price variations for different age groups, group sizes, or time slots.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set capacity">
    Define the maximum number of participants per session. Sailia tracks remaining availability in real time and prevents overbooking.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign staff (optional)">
    Assign [staff members](/staff/management) to the activity. Assigned staff appear on the schedule and their hours feed into [auto timesheets](/staff/auto-timesheets).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add setup and pack-down time (optional)">
    Specify buffer time before and after the activity. This time is included in staff timesheet calculations but not shown to customers.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Capacity and availability

Each activity session has a maximum capacity. As customers book, the available spots decrease in real time. When a session is full:

- The session shows as **sold out** on your booking page
- Customers can join a [waitlist](/activities/waitlists) if you have waitlists enabled
- Walk-in bookings through the [POS](/point-of-sale/overview) also respect capacity limits

<Note>
  Capacity is tracked per session, not per activity. A weekly surf lesson with 10 spots has 10 spots available for each individual session.
</Note>

## Kit and resources

Kit lets you attach physical resources — boats, boards, bikes, wetsuits, helmets — to an activity so that availability is tracked against the resource as well as against the session capacity. When a customer books, Sailia reserves the right quantity of kit automatically.

Each kit item on a session has three settings:

| Setting | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Kit ratio mode** | Whether the ratio is expressed as *people per kit* or *kit per person*. See [Kit ratio modes](#kit-ratio-modes) below. |
| **Kit ratio** | The number used in the ratio (for example, `6`). |
| **Share between bookings** | Whether one item of kit can be split across multiple separate bookings on the same session. |

### Kit ratio modes

A kit ratio describes the relationship between people on the session and items of kit. Select a mode using the tabs above the ratio field:

| Mode | What it means | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **People per kit** | Each item of kit supports a fixed number of people. | A coach boat that seats 6 — pick *People per kit* and enter `6` for *6 people to 1 item of kit*. |
| **Kit per person** | Each person needs a fixed number of items of kit. | A SUP tour where every paddler needs 1 board and 1 paddle — pick *Kit per person* and enter `2` for *2 items of kit to 1 person*. |

Sailia uses the mode to convert between the number of people booked and the number of kit items reserved, so capacity stays accurate however your equipment is allocated. **People per kit** is the default and matches the behaviour of existing kit configurations.

<Note>
  If a session has multiple kit items, each item can use its own ratio mode. For example, a sailing lesson could have boats configured as *people per kit* (6 sailors per boat) and buoyancy aids as *kit per person* (1 aid per sailor).
</Note>

### Share between bookings

The **Share between bookings** setting controls whether a single item of kit can be reserved by more than one booking on the same session:

- **Yes** — kit is shared. A 6-person boat can be filled by two separate bookings of 3 people each.
- **No** — kit is exclusive to the booking that reserves it. A boat booked by one customer is unavailable to others, even if seats remain.

Hire-type activities default to **No** (exclusive), and course-type activities default to **Yes** (shared). You can override the default per kit item.

### Add kit to a session

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the session">
    Open the activity and select the session you want to configure.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a kit item">
    In the **Resources** area, select **Add Kit** and choose the kit from the list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a ratio mode">
    Use the tabs to pick **People per kit** or **Kit per person**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the ratio">
    Enter the number for the ratio. The help text below the field updates to show the full ratio in plain English (for example, *5 people to 1 item of kit*).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose sharing behaviour">
    Set **Share between bookings** to **Yes** or **No** depending on whether the kit can be split across bookings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the session">
    Save the session. Future availability is recalculated using the new ratio and mode.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Changing the ratio or ratio mode on an existing session triggers a recalculation of how much kit each future booking holds. Existing reservations are adjusted automatically so capacity matches the new configuration.
</Tip>

## Minimum notice period

The minimum notice period controls how far in advance customers must book a session. Once the notice period has passed, the session no longer appears on your booking page.

To set a notice period, open an activity and configure three options:

| Setting | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Notice period value** | The amount of lead time required (for example, `2`). |
| **Notice period unit** | The time unit — `days`, `hours`, or `minutes`. |
| **Measured from** | Whether the cutoff is calculated from the session **start time** or the session **end time**. |

For example, if you set a notice period of 24 hours measured from the start time, a session starting at 10:00 AM on Saturday stops accepting online bookings at 10:00 AM on Friday.

If you choose to measure from the end time instead, the cutoff is calculated backwards from when the session finishes. This is useful for longer hires or multi-hour activities where you want to allow bookings closer to the start time.

<Note>
  The notice period uses your business timezone. Make sure your [timezone is set correctly](/getting-started/account-settings#timezone) so the cutoff is calculated accurately for your local time.
</Note>

<Tip>
  Staff and operators are not affected by the notice period — they can still create bookings through the dashboard or POS after the cutoff has passed.
</Tip>

## Add-ons

Add-ons are optional extras that customers can purchase alongside an activity booking. Examples include equipment hire, photography packages, or meal upgrades.

When creating an add-on:

1. Give it a name, description, and price.
2. Link it to one or more activities.
3. Choose whether it is per-person or per-booking.

Add-ons appear during the checkout flow and are included in the order total. They are also eligible for [discount codes](/pricing/discount-codes) if configured.

## Accounting categories

If you use the [Xero accounting sync](/integrations/accounting-sync), assign an accounting category to each activity. This determines how revenue from the activity is categorized on Xero invoices, including the correct VAT rate and account code.

Add-ons are categorized independently of the activities they are linked to. Assign each add-on its own accounting category so that revenue from the add-on is reported under the correct VAT rate and Xero account code, rather than inheriting the parent activity's category. This is important when an add-on (for example, equipment hire or a meal upgrade) has a different VAT treatment from the activity it is sold with.

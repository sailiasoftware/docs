Your terms and conditions are shown to customers at checkout and when they create an account. They cover the legal agreement between your business and the customer — including booking, payment, cancellation, and refund policies.

## Update your T&Cs

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Business settings">
    Go to **Settings → Business** and find the **Terms and conditions** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste or write your terms">
    Use the editor to enter your full terms. You can add headings, lists, and links.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Saved terms are shown to every customer at checkout from that point on. Existing customers are not re-prompted.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Where the T&Cs appear

| Location | When they're shown |
| --- | --- |
| **Checkout** | A required tick box before payment can be completed |
| **Account sign-up** | When a customer creates a new Sailia account with you |
| **Email templates** | Available as the `tnc` merge field to link to your hosted policy |
| **Booking page footer** | A link in the footer of your public booking page |

<Note>
  T&Cs are stored against your business and apply to every sales channel — online booking page, POS, and admin-created bookings.
</Note>

## Keeping your T&Cs up to date

- Review your terms whenever your cancellation, refund, or membership policy changes.
- If you make a material change, communicate it to existing customers via a [campaign](/customers/campaigns) so they know what's new.
- Avoid placing time-sensitive offers or pricing in your T&Cs — those belong in [discount codes](/pricing/discount-codes) or your booking page copy.

Activities are the core bookable items in Sailia. You create activities to represent anything customers can reserve — from surf lessons and kayak hires to multi-day events. Each activity has its own schedule, pricing, capacity, and staffing configuration.

## Activity types

Sailia supports three types of activities:

| Type | Use case |
| --- | --- |
| **Courses** | Scheduled sessions with a fixed start and end time, such as a beginner surf lesson or a yoga class |
| **Hires** | Equipment or resource rentals for a defined duration, such as kayak hire or wetsuit rental |
| **Events** | One-off or recurring events with attendee management, such as a beach clean-up or open day |

All three types share the same booking and payment flow. The main differences are in how scheduling and capacity work.

## Create an activity

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the activities section">
    Navigate to your activities dashboard and select **Create Activity**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the activity type">
    Select whether this is a course, hire, or event.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the basics">
    Enter the activity name, description, and any images. This information appears on your booking page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure scheduling">
    Set when the activity runs — start times, duration, and recurrence. For hires, define the available time slots.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set pricing">
    Define the base price per person or per booking. You can add price variations for different age groups, group sizes, or time slots.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set capacity">
    Define the maximum number of participants per session. Sailia tracks remaining availability in real time and prevents overbooking.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign staff (optional)">
    Assign [staff members](/staff-and-operations/staff-management) to the activity. Assigned staff appear on the schedule and their hours feed into [auto timesheets](/staff-and-operations/auto-timesheets).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add setup and pack-down time (optional)">
    Specify buffer time before and after the activity. This time is included in staff timesheet calculations but not shown to customers.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Capacity and availability

Each activity session has a maximum capacity. As customers book, the available spots decrease in real time. When a session is full:

- The session shows as **sold out** on your booking page
- Customers can join a [waitlist](/booking-and-sales/waitlists) if you have waitlists enabled
- Walk-in bookings through the [POS](/booking-and-sales/point-of-sale) also respect capacity limits

<Note>
  Capacity is tracked per session, not per activity. A weekly surf lesson with 10 spots has 10 spots available for each individual session.
</Note>

## Add-ons

Add-ons are optional extras that customers can purchase alongside an activity booking. Examples include equipment hire, photography packages, or meal upgrades.

When creating an add-on:

1. Give it a name, description, and price.
2. Link it to one or more activities.
3. Choose whether it is per-person or per-booking.

Add-ons appear during the checkout flow and are included in the order total. They are also eligible for [discount codes](/booking-and-sales/discount-codes) if configured.

## Accounting categories

If you use the [Xero accounting sync](/integrations/accounting-sync), assign an accounting category to each activity. This determines how revenue from the activity is categorized on Xero invoices, including the correct VAT rate and account code.
